# Name: Crowly

The product is **Crowly**. The repo/directory stays `agent-output-inbox` (a plain descriptor); "Crowly" is the brand it ships under.

## Why Crowly

- **Coined from "crow."** *To crow* = to announce, loudly and proudly. A crow/rooster heralds the dawn and wakes you to what the day holds — exactly the "what did my agents bring back for me today" widget.
- The crow/corvid is also the clever **messenger bird**: agents fly out, watch, and come back to report. Crowly is where those reports land — a dedicated home for what they brought back, on the user's own server.
- **Warm, concrete, one breath to say, easy to spell.** Reads as a friendly coinage in the well-worn concrete-word app-naming tradition.
- **Near-empty App Store namespace** — the cleanest availability of every candidate screened.

Style bucket: **coined word** (crow + the friendly `-ly` suffix).

## App Store status — NOT yet claimed

- A public iTunes Search API sweep showed effectively zero conflicts for "Crowly" (2026-06-29).
- **Public search is not authoritative.** App Store Connect enforces name uniqueness more strictly — it also locks names held by *unpublished, removed, or merely reserved* app records that never surface in public search. We hit exactly this with **Raven**: clear in public search, yet already taken when creating a record in App Store Connect. Crowly was chosen partly because its namespace is far emptier, lowering that risk.
- **To claim it:** create a New App record in App Store Connect (requires a paid Apple Developer membership — $99/yr — plus a bundle ID and an SKU). The name is held the moment the record is created; no binary or screenshots required. **Not yet done.**

## Trademark caveat

App Store name uniqueness ≠ trademark rights. Before any commercial launch, run a USPTO clearance search (tmsearch.uspto.gov) in **Class 9 (software)** and **Class 42 (SaaS)**. No obvious "Crowly" tech mark surfaced, but this has not been formally cleared.

## Branding notes

- Market and capitalize as **Crowly**.
- The App Store name field is ≤30 chars and the home screen shows only ~the first 12 characters under the icon — so the bare word "Crowly" carries it; no qualifier needed.
- The crow shows up as **animated onboarding art** (one beat per first-run screen, `docs/ux.md` § Onboarding). As of 2026-07-02 that art is a **SwiftUI placeholder** — the real Lottie `.lottie` crow assets aren't sourced yet, so no finished crow character has shipped.

## Alternatives considered & rejected

- **Raven / RAVN** — strongest concept fit (Odin's ravens Huginn "Thought" and Muninn "Memory" fly out and report back), but **"Raven" is already taken in App Store Connect**, the namespace is crowded (RavenPay, Raven Controls) and sits near the Baltimore Ravens mark; **RAVN** collides with "Ravn: Link Saver" and iManage RAVN (legal AI).
- **Roost** — thematically perfect (outputs "come home to roost") but **encumbered** (Roost smart-home brand + a Productivity "Roost").
- **Round 1 (Greek theme):** Hermes, Iris, Apollo are all taken by same-lane AI/productivity apps; the best available were **Angelia** (Hermes' messenger daughter) and **Behest**.

Full research lives in the conversation that produced this doc — two `deep-research` rounds run on 2026-06-29 (Greek-mythology names, then non-Greek/"rooster"-family names).
